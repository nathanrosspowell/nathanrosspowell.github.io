Poker II Upgrades

Posted: 2014/12/30 22:42

Keyboards are awesome!

Recently, I have dived into the world of mechanical keyboards.

As someone who spends far too much time at a keyboard, both professionaly and personally, it seems only natural to invest in the tools on my desktop. There is definitely a large array of choices for keyboards as long as you are willing to get over the price barrier of what a 'normal' keyboard is worth.

My personal preference for periferals is Logitech. The last keyboard and mouse combo I purchased was the Wave for about £50. It was then a big step to buy a new keyboard from a far smaller, unknown to me, company for $130.

Vortex Poker II

The Poker II is a very popular keyboard among enthusiasts.

  • 60% keyboard
    • drastically reduced size because it only has 60% of the keys of a standard layout
  • Function layers
    • the 'missing' keys are on a function layer
    • just like using the 'shift' on the number row to get a symbol, the 'fn' key
  • Cherry MX switches
    • German made mechanical switches
    • a selection of options for key press feel

The exact keyboard I got was from Massdrop website which is (at the time of writing) the only place to get the keyboard with Cherry MX Clear tactile switches.

DSA Dolch

A bonus to having a large number of mechanical keyboards using the Cherry MX switches is that there's quite a selection of aftermarket key caps to chose from.

On a standard keyboard you have probably noticed that each row is sculpted to form a curve. There are a number of different profiles available on the aftermarket, I went with DSA which is completely flat. The low profile appealed to me and I think it goes well visually with a small footprint keyboard.

The colour choice is more subjective but I, along with many others, fell in love with the Dolch colour scheme. The orange WASD keys are from the 'gamer' pack.

TEX

A new case seems to be even more of a vanity issue than the keycaps - but it does make a difference.

As much as I love the board, it was giving off a crazy amount of ping, especially on the left side on the home row. When replacing the case I cut up an old mouse mat which was about 3mm thick to go in the grooves to dampen the board. This was great in reducing the resonating of the ping sound, but it hasn't removed it completely. After I get a few a bit more experience with mods (I've got a Planck hand wiring kit in the mail), I'm going to rip the whole thing apart and give the springs a good seeing too. Maybe then It will be ping free!

The added weight with the aluminum case does help with it staying still on the desk (I personally haven't ever had problems with a keyboard slipping around). It is a weird sensation but I can feel the weight of the board when I type and it is very solid.
